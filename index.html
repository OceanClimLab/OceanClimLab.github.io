What we do

Reading the ocean's role in climate

The CryOClim-Lab works on the detection and attribution of global and regional ocean warming, sea-level rise, ocean circulation, water-mass changes, and transformations, marine heatwaves, compound extreme events, mesoscale eddies, and the global and regional hydrological cycle.

Our approach combines ocean and climate modelling with machine learning, drawing on observations, reanalyses, and model simulations to ask how the ocean stores heat, moves water and energy around the planet, and drives the extremes communities feel on land. We are based at the Centre for Atmospheric Sciences, IIT Delhi.

Focus areas

  • Ocean warming
  • Sea-level rise
  • Ocean circulation
  • Cryosphere Change and Variability
  • Water masses
  • Marine heatwaves
  • Compound extremes
  • Mesoscale eddies
  • Hydrological cycle
  • Climate modelling
  • Machine learning
